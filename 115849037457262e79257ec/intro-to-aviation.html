Intro to Aviation

Thomas Scott Baldwin, n.d.
During Curtiss’ successful career as a motorcycle racer, he was approached by a dirigible flyer named Thomas Baldwin to make engines for his dirigibles. Despite the Wright Brothers having flown heavier-than-air machines, lighter-than-air dirigibles dominated the aviation industry, and very few people had seen a Wright Flyer in flight. [6] Curtiss and Baldwin’s partnership resulted in prizes and acclaim for Baldwin’s dirigibles, introducing Glenn Curtiss to the fledgling world of aviation.
Thomas Scott Baldwin's airship at the St. Louis Exposition, 1904.
Image of Baldwin in the Hammondsport Herald's Account of Success of Captain Baldwin's Dirigible, Nov. 30, 1904.
Hammondsport Herald's Account of Success of Curtiss Factory after Baldwin's St. Louis Exposition, Jan. 4, 1905.
In May 1906, Curtiss encountered the Wright Brothers in Dayton, OH, while attending a fair. Curtiss proposed to the brothers that they could use his engines, having a long and amicable conversation in their shop. In the end, they declined, preferring their own custom-built engines.
Later that year, Alexander Graham Bell assembled a team of young men to aid him in experimenting with flight, creating the Aerial Experiment Association. Dr. Bell met Curtiss in 1907 and asked him to join the team due to his expertise in designing engines.
